   1 \section{Tooltip placement}
   2 \label{sec:tooltip_placement}
   3 
   4 
   5 The placement of the GUI2 tooltips are handled by the tooltip window. This
   6 window needs to determine where it wants the tooltip to be placed, and
   7 determine its optimal size.
   8 
   9 \begin{description}
  10 \item[\cref{sec:tooltip_placement:normal}]
  11         Shows the how normal tooltips look in the game window.
  12 
  13 \item[\cref{sec:tooltip_placement:large}]
  14         Shows the how large tooltips look in the game window.
  15 
  16 \item[\cref{sec:tooltip_placement:huge}]
  17         Shows the how huge tooltips look in the game window.
  18 
  19 \end{description}
  20 
  21 
  22 \subsection{Normal}
  23 \label{sec:tooltip_placement:normal}
  24 
  25 This tooltip has a single line of text and the width of the tooltip is below or
  26 equal to the wanted width of the tooltip. The wanted width of the tooltip is
  27 determined by a size where the line-length of the text is pleasant to read.
  28 
  29 Or the  tooltip has either a end of line character, causing it to be spread
  30 over several lines, or its text is too long for the wanted width, causing it to
  31 be wrapped. The text's height will still fit on the screen.
  32 
  33 \Cref{fig:tooltip_placement} gives a visual representation of the placement
  34 algorithms used. The algorithms used are:
  35 \begin{enumerate}[I.]
  36 \item\label{sec:tooltip_placement:algorithm:I}
  37         This is the preferred placement of the tooltip. The lower right corner of
  38         the tooltip is attached to the top left of the mouse pointer.
  39 
  40 \item\label{sec:tooltip_placement:algorithm:II}
  41         This is used when the mouse \mbox{x-position} is greater than the width of
  42         the tooltip window, but the mouse \mbox{y-position} is smaller than the
  43         height of the tooltip window. The top of the tooltip is attached to the top
  44         of the screen at the left side of the mouse pointer.
  45 
  46 \item\label{sec:tooltip_placement:algorithm:III}
  47         This is used when the mouse \mbox{x-position} is smaller than the width of
  48         the tooltip window, but the mouse \mbox{x-position} is greater than the
  49         height of the tooltip window. The left side of the tooltip is attached to
  50         the left side of the screen at the top of the mouse pointer.
  51 
  52 \item\label{sec:tooltip_placement:algorithm:IV}
  53         This is used when the mouse \mbox{x-position} is smaller than the width of
  54         the tooltip window, and the mouse \mbox{y-position} is smaller than the
  55         height of the tooltip window. The top of the tooltip is attached to the top
  56         of the screen at the right side of the mouse pointer.
  57 
  58 \end{enumerate}

  98 \subsection{Large}
  99 \label{sec:tooltip_placement:large}
 100 
 101 This tooltip needs so much space that its height no longer fits on the
 102 screen. In this case the width will be increased. There is no guarantee the
 103 tooltip will fit properly; the creator of the tooltip should consider
 104 whether or not it contains too much information. Once the width is adjusted the
 105 placement algorithm used it the same as for the normal tooltip, see
 106 \cref{sec:tooltip_placement:normal}.
 107 
 108 
 109 \subsection{Huge}
 110 \label{sec:tooltip_placement:huge}
 111 
 112 The tooltip will certainly not fit nicely on the screen and no attempt is
 113 made to even try to do something sane. Instead it is placed in the top left
 114 corner. Unless a very small screen resolution is used the creator of the
 115 tooltip really needs to consider reducing the amount of text on the tooltip.
 116 There is no guarantee the entire tooltip is visible.
