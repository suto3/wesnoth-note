  17     # This campaign, as originally written, had no definite time or
  18     # place.  There was a vague reference to "the eastern reaches of
  19     # the kingdom of Wesnoth".  In a later revision the epilogue hints
  20     # that Arvith may have traveled with the elf-lord Kalenz, but this is
  21     # not much of a constraint as Kalenz was around in 1YW and at
  22     # least until the beginning of HttT in 517 YW.
  23     #
  24     # Maghre has to be within reach of the orcs, but in a time and
  25     # place where there haven't been orc raids for generations. I (ESR)
  26     # think this limits us to *western* Wesnoth, orc raids having been a
  27     # chronic problem in the northeast even during the Kingdom's strongest
  28     # periods. The logical time would be shortly after the death of
  29     # Haldric IV in 350, the beginning of the First Dark Age.  I'm arbitrarily
  30     # setting it at 363; Kalenz began his wanderings ten years later, so the
  31     # timing for Arvith to meet him later in life isn't bad.  (Later moved
  32     # to 363 so the timing of the Epilogue. A-Red's backstory changes works
  33     # better.)
  34 
  35     # Placing the campaign is a little trickier.  Our main textual
  36     # clue is that there is a dense wood, said by legend to be
  37     # haunted, a few days' ride north.  Later, the boss's keep is said to
  38     # be still further north.  Neither of these directions has plot importance,
  39     # but they do suggest a locale for Maghre - near the Grey Woods.  The
  40     # battle maps exclude it from being in a featureless plain, though;
  41     # there are hills to the south and west.
  42     #
  43     # So let it be written, so let it be done.  See the tracking map
  44     # for the result.
